Pint sized and Baptised: Why I got dunked?

A story about faith, questions, and baptism.

I have something to admit to you.

I failed my driving test 3 times, which is pretty embarrassing, I know. There is only one person I’ve met who has attempted the same feat. I won’t name them.

I can confidently say that I have never been more nervous than on those nights before a driving test. The second most nervous I have ever been, however, was when I was sitting in the car with my Dad – a deeply rooted atheist – and telling him “I think I am a Christian”.

To my Dad, why would I, someone whom he brought up well, never got in real trouble, want anything to do with Christianity?

Let me take you back to the summer of 2024, where if you had asked me how my life was going, I would’ve responded “Brilliantly!”

I had good grades, holidays, money, steak, like lots of steak.

Once, I took my friend Leon out for a steak. Leon, by the way, is a training actor, and so money wasn’t his best friend. When it came to paying, I told Leon, “It’s on me”. Man, that felt good.

That summer ended with me being unsatisfied, because even with all the things a 19-year-old man could want, I was still hungry for more.

I would now like to introduce you to a quote:

“You may be the only bible someone ever reads”

When the second year of University rolled around, I met a very interesting one.

And for the first time in my life, I started asking questions about Jesus,

The first was this:

Did Jesus actually exist?

This one was pretty easy to answer, but what provided me with concrete evidence?

John 20:3-4

For some context, John was one of Jesus’ mates and wrote an eyewitness account of Jesus’ life. At this point in the account, he and Jesus’ other mates have been made aware that Jesus is not in the tomb, and John writes this:

“So Peter and the other disciple started for the tomb. Both were running, but the other disciple (John) outran Peter and reached the tomb first.”

John, in the third person, has pointed out that he was faster than Peter.

If you were making up a story, why would you add that in? You can argue with me later about the concreteness of my argument.

The next question was a little bit more complicated.

Was Jesus God?

`

Well, he said that he was.

If we go back to John’s account, Jesus is quoted as saying.

"Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father (God)"

Jesus, whom I only just accepted as being real, said that?

But he also proves to be.

I had the pleasure of watching a testimony at St Nicholas Church in Bristol, where a young man, addicted to drugs, turned to Jesus. The moment he did, he was completely set free from his addiction and lives an extraordinary life now. How incredible.

Now, I am in a lucky position to not have been addicted to drugs, but let me tell you what I was addicted to:

Jealousy, money, hate, gossip, lust.

When I invited Jesus into my life, he set me free from my addictions.

So why did I get baptised?

Well, for one, Jesus, whom I have now considered as God, told us to. But more than that, it’s what it represents.

Firstly, I got to publicly declare my belief to friends, family and the church. What a joy it was to share.

Secondly, the symbolic nature represents a change in me. The act of going under and coming back up from the water symbolises dying to my old self, a washing away and then being born anew.

May 16th 2024, I ran the Bristol half.

May 5th 2025, I ran the Mk marathon

May 10th 2026, I got baptised.

And that’s the most important one.
