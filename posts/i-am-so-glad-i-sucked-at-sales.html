A story about work, friends, and finding out what the wrong path teaches you.

I am so glad I sucked at sales.

My parents didn't go to University. In fact, no one in my family, besides my aunt, has gone to university.

I almost didn't either.

You see, at the ripe age of 17, I had no influence around University. Moreover, I was influenced by my parents to do quite the opposite.

"Why get yourself £50,000 in debt?"

Great question, I thought, because I didn't have the answer.

My sixth form required all students to apply.

So, having visited a grand total of no universities, I googled:

"Universities with the best nightlife".

Nottingham, Bristol, Leeds, Newcastle & Liverpool.

These became my 5 choices.

This utterly poor university search culminated on results day, when I didn't get into my top choice (Nottingham, not for the University, but for the city's other famous attributes) due to my, admittedly, unfavourable A levels.

Luckily for me, 2 of my good friends, Chris and Leon, were also handed results further down the alphabet (Sorry for throwing you both under the bus) .

My backup, the University of the West of England, had accepted me.

I deferred the offer.

With a year now in our hands, Chris, Leon and I put our heads together.

Our plan? Find out if we really needed university. For 2/3 of us, we did.

Amidst the travelling (as is customary for Buckinghamshire's youth on a gap year), the 3 of us acquired some rather "classy" jobs. An ice cream server with a knack for acting (Leon) , a car salesman (Chris), and a telemarketer (Myself).

Telesales sucked.

With an "On Target Earning" of £50,000 a year, University made even less sense.

I left the job after a month.

You probably aren't surprised to hear that I didn't do very well at sales. It definitely takes a certain type of person. That person is Chris.

I have much admiration for Chris. He is still selling cars to this day, and I have a good feeling that OTE is being hit.

One time, Leon and I went into the dealership Chris worked at to poke fun at him, of course. We almost left with a new Ford Fiesta.

As for Leon, I think he enjoyed his time at the ice cream shop. His real passion was for acting, however. He is due to graduate from a prestigious drama school in the next few months. I'm sure there is a long list of agents waiting to snap him up once he is released from his alma mater.

I spent the rest of that year working at Tesco, solemn in the fact that I was going to University in the coming September.

This became the best choice I ever made.

Theme

Sometimes the failed experiment is the thing that makes the decision clear.
