Do you have the Greggs app?

A sausage roll, a drive through window, and the tiny frictions we accept in exchange for rewards.

Last week I seriously considered my life choices when I found myself at the drive through window of a Greggs.

"Just a sausage roll please mate," I asked reluctantly.

Pulling up to the collection window, I got asked a familiar question.

"Do you have the Greggs app?"

Well I've got an app to track my shop at Tesco, and Lidl.

One to record my runs.

Another to log what I eat in a day.

Another still to locate my family at all times.

Oh, and even one to collect points for McDonalds.

But the Greggs app?

This one had escaped me so far.

"No, sorry," I respond.

"Well did you know that you can unlock great rewards and save on -"

"Honestly mate, it's okay."

I drove off, sausage roll in hand.

Biting into it, with a slight sense of buyer's remorse, I thought:

Had I just missed out on 1.3 Greggs points?!

Just 19 more sausage rolls and I get a free steak bake!

As someone who is interested in building products, it made me wonder:

Who is benefitting from this transaction, or are we just adding more friction in exchange for data?

Curious what you think.
